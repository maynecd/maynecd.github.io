Best Practices for Advanced Imaging Orders

COMPETENCY: Front-End Analysis and System Evaluation: To provide students with experiences to develop skills in data c ollection, analysis and evaluation for reflecting on and applying to the design/development of learning opportunities and systems.

Course: IS_LT 9471 Instructional System Design

Project Description: This group project required the identification of a problem that could be solved through the implementation of a training program. The project required our team to analyze the needs for the training, deconstruct the performance required for success after training, and then we designed the training experience. The elements of this project included needs analysis, learner analysis, task analysis, content analysis, gap analysis, contextual analysis, training design, assessment, implementation, evaluation, and a working prototype.

Artifacts:

Best Practices for Advanced Imaging Orders

This team project consisted of four individuals who are in differing healthcare fields, but have identified an overlapping issue within their career environments pertaining to errant advanced imaging orders. The link provided shows the final prototype for module 1. I served as the project lead and oversaw the project deliverables and timelines. This project represents the competency above through the representation of the two objectives listed above.

Reflection: This project provided me invaluable experiences pertaining to the instructional design process and helped me to understand the challenges and nuances of the process. The project left me with strong foundational knowledge of the conceptual frameworks of ID. The members within our team were driven, motivated, and goal-oriented, which was a definite strength. One of the weaknesses within the group was the varying levels of knowledge pertaining to the subject matter, but those gaps were identified early and able to be resolved through logical task delegation.
