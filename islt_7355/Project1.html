Rise of the Botanical Force Digital Game

COMPETENCY: Design and Development of Learning Systems-Student is able to design and develop innovative, aesthetic, effective, and sustainable design/development and management of technologies for learning opportunities and systems.

Course: IS_LT 7384 Designing Games for Learning

Project Description:

This project was a team project that led to the design and development of digital learning game using Construct 3. This project required several variables and was critiqued on the following elements: knowledge domain, learning objective(s), game goal, core dynamic, mechanics, assessment/scoring/rewards, core loop, and the production of a working prototype. The project design required application and justification for how the game addressed a minimum of 2 Geeâ€™s principles of learning in games.

Artifacts:

Rise of the Botanical Force

This team project design provides players an educational opportunity embedded within gameplay to interact with virtual gardening and hybridization. During gameplay, players must explore the game environment to examine and collect resources to create and grow hybrid breeds of roses all while being exposed to potential threats that may interrupt their success with propagating new plant species. The workload was split evenly with my portion being heavily devoted to research, content development, and articulating the various elements for submission on the game design document. This project represents the competency above through the representation of the three objectives listed above.

Reflection:

The greatest strength of this project was teamwork; the pairing of two people who are creative, driven, and goal-oriented. Construct 3 provided numerous opportunities for learning and experimentation, which was a wonderful experience. During the project debriefing, we discussed our approach beginning with our initial brainstorming session through the final submission and agreed that if we were to do the process over, we would not change anything because our process simply worked and I believe that can be attributed to our academic styles and personal motivations.
